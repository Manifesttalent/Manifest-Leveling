What is leveling and why does it matter?

Leveling describes the scope, complexity, and impact expected at each career stage. It is not about tenure — it is about the kind of problems you solve, how independently you solve them, and how wide your impact reaches.

The 7 criteria

Two tracks
IC vs Manager

Moving into management is not a promotion — it is a different role. Strong ICs can reach IC4 without managing anyone.

Individual Contributor (IC)

IC1.1 > IC1.2 > IC2.1 > IC2.2 > IC3.1 > IC3.2 > IC4.1 > IC4.2

Impact grows through depth and scope of expertise. People Leadership is never a prerequisite.

People Manager (M)

M1.1 > M1.2 > M2.1 > M2.2 > M3.1 > M3.2 > M4.1 > M4.2 > M5 > M6

Impact grows through teams built and outcomes owned. Entry requires IC2.2 minimum; IC3.1 preferred.

PL cap: why it is the only DR-constrained criterion
Manager split logic

Only People Leadership is hard-capped by DR count. All other criteria (Scope, Complexity, Autonomy, Hard Skills, Culture) use manager anchors at full weight — a lean manager can genuinely own company-level scope.

1-2 DRs → PL max 1"Leads 1-3 with support." This is the anchor definition — you literally cannot score higher with 1-2 DRs.
3-5 DRs → PL max 2"Manages 4-8, runs 1:1s, hiring." Achievable and verifiable at this team size.
6-9 DRs → PL max 3"Leads managers or multiple squads." Requires this team size to demonstrate in practice.
10+ DRs → PL max 4"Builds bench, fixes org design." Only demonstrable at scale.

Interview signals

📐 Scope & Impact

Largest initiative owned end-to-end. Specific metric outcomes: %, time saved, cost reduced.

🧩 Complexity

Where they simplified something messy. Reduced handoffs, resolved cross-team blockers, systems others reuse.

🧭 Autonomy & Ownership

A time they had no playbook. Proactive risk ID, decisions without escalation.

⚡ Hard Skills

Real functional scenario. Depth of diagnosis: can they teach back, review others' work, invent new methods?

🤝 Culture & Behaviors

Owned a mistake publicly or raised a standard others did not meet. Consistent patterns, not one story.

👥 People Leadership

For lean managers: quality of 1:1s and whether DRs are growing. For full managers: attrition, performance cases, succession depth.
