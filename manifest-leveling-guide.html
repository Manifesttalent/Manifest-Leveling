Career Leveling Guide

How roles, titles, and expectations are structured across Manifest — for candidates discovering us, managers calibrating hires, and employees navigating their growth.

What is leveling and why does it matter?

Leveling is how we describe the scope, complexity, and impact expected at each stage of a career at Manifest. It's not about tenure — it's about the kind of problems you solve, how independently you solve them, and how wide your impact reaches. Every role, every hire, and every promotion decision maps back to this framework.

The 7 criteria
What we assess

Every role is evaluated across these dimensions. The weight and expectation shifts as you move up — and for people managers, People Leadership becomes a core criterion rather than optional.

📐

Scope & Impact

How wide is your reach? From completing assigned tasks inside one pod, to driving company-critical initiatives with P&L impact.

1 · Task-level 2 · Process-owner 3 · Cross-team 4 · Function-wide 5 · Company-level
🧩

Complexity

How hard are the problems? From following clear SOPs with low ambiguity, to designing patterns the whole organisation adopts.

1 · Recipes/SOPs 2 · Routine fixes 3 · Simplifies flows 4 · Durable systems 5 · New paradigms
🧭

Autonomy & Ownership

How independently do you operate? From needing close guidance, to being a zero-surprise operator others plan around.

1 · Guided 2 · Self-directed 3 · End-to-end delivery 4 · Strategy-setter 5 · Standard-setter
⚡

Hard Skills

How deep is your technical or functional expertise? From basic tool usage, to inventing techniques and being an industry authority.

1 · Basic tools 2 · Executes alone 3 · Improves systems 4 · Expert teacher 5 · Industry-level
🤝

Culture & Behaviors

How consistently do you embody our values? From needing reminders, to being a culture carrier under pressure across the organisation.

1 · Needs reminders 2 · Usually follows 3 · Role model 4 · Raises the bar 5 · Culture carrier
👥

People Leadership

Not expected at IC levels, but rewarded. For managers, this is the core criterion — from leading 2–5 directs to developing future executives.

IC · Not required M1 · 2–8 directs M2 · Leads leads M3–4 · VP/Director layer M5–6 · C-Level bench
📅

Years of Experience

A supporting signal, not the deciding factor. Used to sense-check — not to promote automatically. Experience alone does not unlock a level.

1 · 0–2 yrs 2 · 3–5 yrs 3 · 6–10 yrs 4 · 10–14 yrs 5 · 15+ yrs
Two tracks
IC vs Manager

Manifest has two parallel career tracks. Moving into management is not a promotion — it's a different role. Strong ICs can progress to IC4 (Principal / Senior Principal) without managing people.

Individual Contributor (IC)

IC1.1 → IC1.2 → IC2.1 → IC2.2 → IC3.1 → IC3.2 → IC4.1 → IC4.2

Your impact grows through depth of expertise and scope of influence — not through managing people. IC4s are principal-level experts who shape function or company direction. People Leadership is never a prerequisite on this track.

People Manager (M)

M1.1 → M1.2 → M2.1 → M2.2 → M3.1 → M3.2 → M4.1 → M4.2 → M5 → M6

Your impact grows through the teams you build and the function-level outcomes you own. From your first team lead role to C-Level, each step adds span, P&L accountability, and leadership depth. People Leadership is a core scored criterion from M1.1 upward.

What to look for in interviews
Criterion interview signals

Each criterion maps to specific evidence types you should probe for.

📐

Scope & Impact

Ask about the largest initiative they owned end-to-end. Look for specificity on metric outcomes — %, time, cost. Vague impact claims signal a lower score.

🧩

Complexity

Ask where they've simplified something messy. Look for examples of reducing handoffs, resolving cross-team blockers, or designing systems others reuse.

🧭

Autonomy & Ownership

Ask about a time they had no playbook. Look for proactive risk identification, decision-making without escalation, and "zero surprises" operating patterns.

⚡

Hard Skills

Give a real functional scenario. Look for depth of diagnosis, not just the right answer — can they teach back, review others' work, invent new methods?

🤝

Culture & Behaviors

Ask about a time they owned a mistake publicly, or raised a standard others didn't meet. Look for consistency — not just values-aligned stories but patterns.

👥

People Leadership (Manager roles)

Ask about attrition they've managed, performance cases, succession plans. Look for proactive bench-building, not reactive firefighting.
