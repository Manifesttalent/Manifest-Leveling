What is leveling and why does it matter?

Leveling describes the scope, complexity, and impact expected at each career stage. It is not about tenure. It is about the kind of problems you solve, how independently you solve them, and how wide your impact reaches. Every role, hire, and promotion maps back to this framework.

Two tracks
IC vs Manager

Moving into management is not a promotion — it is a different role. Strong ICs can reach IC4 without managing anyone.

Individual Contributor (IC)

IC1.1 > IC1.2 > IC2.1 > IC2.2 > IC3.1 > IC3.2 > IC4.1 > IC4.2

Impact grows through depth and scope. IC4s shape function or company direction without managing people.

People Manager (M)

M1.1 > M1.2 > M2.1 > M2.2 > M3.1 > M3.2 > M4.1 > M4.2 > M5 > M6

Impact grows through teams built and outcomes owned. Entry requires IC2.2 minimum; IC3.1 preferred.

Lean manager context
Managers with 1-2 direct reports

Manifest is lean. Managers with small teams remain highly hands-on. Assessment reflects actual role split, not a theoretical full-manager model.

Nominal (1 DR)15% mgmt / 85% IC. PL is a minor factor. Primarily assessed as a senior IC with a formal line responsibility.
Lean (2 DRs)25% mgmt / 75% IC. PL starts to matter but IC depth still dominates.
Hybrid (3-4 DRs)45% mgmt / 55% IC. Balanced. Both IC depth and people leadership carry equal weight.
Manager-Primary (5-7 DRs)60% mgmt / 40% IC. People leadership and scope dominate.
Full Manager (8+ DRs)75% mgmt / 25% IC. Assessment dominated by leadership, team outcomes, and organisational scope.

Interview signals
What to probe per criterion

📐 Scope & Impact

Largest initiative owned end-to-end. Specific metric outcomes: %, time saved, cost reduced. Vague impact claims signal lower score.

🧩 Complexity

Where they simplified something messy. Reduced handoffs, resolved cross-team blockers, or built systems others now reuse.

🧭 Autonomy & Ownership

A time they had no playbook. Proactive risk identification, decisions without escalation, zero-surprises patterns.

⚡ Hard Skills

Real functional scenario. Depth of diagnosis: can they teach back, review others' work, invent new methods?

🤝 Culture & Behaviors

Owned a mistake publicly, or raised a standard others did not meet. Consistent patterns, not just one good story.

👥 People Leadership

For lean managers: quality of 1:1s, whether DRs are growing. For full managers: attrition, performance cases, succession depth.
