Division:

What is leveling and why does it matter?

Leveling describes the scope, complexity, and impact expected at each career stage at Manifest. It is not about tenure. It is about the kind of problems you solve, how independently you solve them, and how wide your impact reaches. Every role, hire, and promotion maps back to this framework.

Two tracks
IC vs Manager

Manifest has two parallel career tracks. Moving into management is not a promotion — it is a different role. Strong ICs can reach IC4 without managing anyone.

Individual Contributor (IC)

IC1.1 > IC1.2 > IC2.1 > IC2.2 > IC3.1 > IC3.2 > IC4.1 > IC4.2

Impact grows through depth of expertise and scope of influence. People Leadership is never a prerequisite on this track.

People Manager (M)

M1.1 > M1.2 > M2.1 > M2.2 > M3.1 > M3.2 > M4.1 > M4.2 > M5 > M6

Impact grows through teams built and outcomes owned. People Leadership is core from M1.1 upward. Entry requires IC2.2 minimum; IC3.1 is preferred.

Lean manager context
Managers with 1-2 direct reports

Manifest is a lean organisation. Managers with small teams remain highly hands-on as individual contributors. Assessment should reflect the actual role split, not a theoretical management-heavy model.

Nominal Manager (1 DR)15% management, 85% IC. People Leadership is a minor factor. Primarily assessed as a senior IC with a formal line management responsibility.
Lean Manager (2 DRs)25% management, 75% IC. Still primarily IC-assessed, but people leadership signals start to matter.
Hybrid Manager (3-4 DRs)45% management, 55% IC. Balanced. Both IC depth and people leadership carry equal weight.
Manager-Primary (5-7 DRs)60% management, 40% IC. People leadership and scope dominate. IC criteria remain but carry less weight.
Full Manager (8+ DRs)75% management, 25% IC. Assessment is dominated by leadership, team outcomes, and organisational scope.

Interview signals
What to probe per criterion

📐 Scope & Impact

Ask about the largest initiative they owned end-to-end. Look for specific metric outcomes: %, time saved, cost reduced. Vague impact claims signal a lower score.

🧩 Complexity

Ask where they simplified something messy. Look for reduced handoffs, resolved cross-team blockers, or systems others now reuse.

🧭 Autonomy & Ownership

Ask about a time they had no playbook. Look for proactive risk identification, decisions without escalation, zero-surprises operating patterns.

⚡ Hard Skills

Give a real functional scenario. Look for depth of diagnosis: can they teach back, review others' work, invent new methods?

🤝 Culture & Behaviors

Ask about owning a mistake publicly, or raising a standard others did not meet. Look for consistent patterns, not just one good story.

👥 People Leadership

For lean managers: ask about the quality of 1:1s and whether their DRs are growing, not just headcount managed. For full managers: attrition, performance cases, succession depth.
