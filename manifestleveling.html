What is leveling and why does it matter?

Leveling describes the scope, complexity, and impact expected at each career stage. It is not about tenure: it is about the kind of problems you solve, how independently you solve them, and how wide your impact reaches.

The 7 criteria
What we assess

Every role is evaluated across these dimensions. For managers, People Leadership becomes core and is automatically capped based on actual team size.

Two tracks
IC vs Manager

Both paths are equally valid and respected at Manifest. Moving into management is a promotion — and so is deepening your craft as an IC. The difference is in what you are optimising for: building through people, or building through expertise. Neither path is a prerequisite for the other.

Individual Contributor (IC)

IC1.1 → IC1.2 → IC2.1 → IC2.2 → IC3.1 → IC3.2 → IC4.1 → IC4.2

Impact grows through depth, scope, and the quality of what you build. You can reach the most senior IC levels purely through craft and influence — no team required.

People Manager (M)

M1.1 → M1.2 → M2.1 → M2.2 → M3.1 → M3.2 → M4.1 → M4.2 → M5

Impact grows through the quality of the team you build and the outcomes they own. Entry requires IC2.2 minimum — IC3.1 preferred. The bar is readiness, not tenure.

For hiring, promotions & career conversations
Level Assessment

Score against the leveling criteria - use for calibrating new hires, assessing current employees, or career conversations. Note: Years of Experience is a contextual signal, not a gate. A candidate can score IC3.1 with 3 years if the evidence supports it.

Manager track eligibilityCandidates can only enter M1.1+ if their IC-equivalent capability is IC2.2 or IC3.1. Candidates below IC2.2 cannot be placed into a manager role.

Quick level estimator

Select a track, then rate 1 to 5 on each criterion using the anchor text as your calibration guide. Score what you can prove with evidence, not what feels right.

Interview signals

📐 Scope & Impact

Largest initiative owned end-to-end. Specific metric outcomes: %, time saved, cost reduced.

🧩 Complexity

Where they simplified something messy. Reduced handoffs, resolved cross-team blockers, systems others reuse.

🧭 Autonomy & Ownership

A time they had no playbook. Proactive risk ID, decisions without escalation.

⚡ Hard Skills

Real functional scenario. Depth of diagnosis: can they teach back, review others' work, invent new methods?

🤝 Culture & Behaviors

Owned a mistake publicly or raised a standard others did not meet. Consistent patterns, not one story.

👥 People Leadership

For lean managers: quality of 1:1s and whether DRs are growing. For full managers: attrition, performance cases, succession depth.
